Fried Chicken

Fried chicken is a popular dish made by coating chicken pieces in seasoned flour or batter and deep-frying them until they are crispy and golden brown.
The result is a flavorful and crunchy exterior with juicy and tender meat on the inside.
Fried chicken is often served with sides like mashed potatoes, coleslaw, or biscuits, making it a beloved comfort food in many cultures.

Crispy Fried Chicken Recipe

Chicken drumsticks are always a favorite around here. You'll love the delightful flavor of
these fried drumsticks seasoned with paprika, onion powder, garlic powder, and black
pepper with a dash of chili powder for added flavor

These fried chicken drumsticks are an excellent addition to any picnic, family gathering,
weeknight dinner, or a special treat. You can serve this fried chicken recipe as a
standalone treat or with your favorite sides for dinner like potato salad, coleslaw,
mashed potatoes, and so much more.

How Do I make A Flavorful Crispy Fried Chicken Legs?

This recipe uses whole milk and eggs as wet ingredients. However, you can use buttermilk in
place of whole milk if that's what you prefer.

The dry ingredients are flour and spices and there's no need to marinate the chicken as
long as you season your milk mixture with salt and black pepper. If you don't season it,
you will miss out on flavor. Trust me on this!

If you follow these easy steps your fried chicken will turn out into an outer shell
that you love biting into, with a moist and tender interior so be sure not to
skip any step.

For the best results, use a deep-fryer or a large heavy-bottomed pot to fry your chicken.
This will help maintain a consistent temperature and ensure even cooking.
Use a thermometer to monitor the oil temperature, keeping it between 350°F (175°C)
and 375°F (190°C) for optimal frying. Avoid overcrowding the pot, as this can lower the oil temperature
and result in greasy chicken. Fry the chicken in batches if necessary, allowing enough space for
each piece to cook evenly.

Crispy Fried Chicken Drumsticks

First, make a mixture of milk, eggs, salt, and black pepper. Set aside.

Next, combine the flour with the spices. Mix well.

Place the legs inside the dry ingredient mixture and coat well.
Then transfer it to the milk mixture. Coat well.

Finally, transfer the legs back to the bowl of dry ingredients and
coat well until the legs become thickly coated. At this point, the
chicken is ready to be fried.

If you want the pieces extra crispy, redip in the egg-milk mixture and seasoned flour.

Fry these battered chicken legs preheated oil in it. The oil in the pan must reach 350
degrees for optimal crisping of the fried chicken batter. You'll turn the chicken over
partway through the frying process until the entire drumstick is crisp and browned—about
12 to 15 minutes.

Be sure to place your fried chicken on a plate lined with paper towels to soak up the
extra oil and allow your fried chicken rest for 1-2 minutes before serving.


How long does it take to fry Chicken Legs?

An average chicken leg will take approximately 12 to 15 minutes when deep pan-frying,
but it's also advisable to check the chicken's internal temperature to ensure the
meat is cooked to 165 degrees Fahrenheit.

Why is my fried chicken not crispy?

If your fried chicken isn't crispy, it could be due to several factors:
1. Oil Temperature: Ensure the oil is at the right temperature (350-375°F or 175-190°C)
before adding the chicken. If the oil is too cool, the chicken will absorb more oil and become greasy rather than crispy.
2. Overcrowding: Avoid overcrowding the pan, as this can lower the oil temperature and lead to soggy chicken.
Fry in batches if necessary.
3. Coating: Make sure the chicken is well-coated with a seasoned flour mixture. A thicker coating can help achieve a crispier texture.
4. Draining: After frying, place the chicken on a wire rack instead of paper towels to drain excess oil. This prevents the chicken from sitting in oil and becoming soggy.
5. Resting: Allow the chicken to rest for a few minutes after frying to let the coating set and become crispier.
6. Type of Flour: Using all-purpose flour is common, but mixing in some cornstarch can enhance crispiness.
7. Moisture: Pat the chicken dry before coating it to remove excess moisture, which can prevent the coating from adhering properly and becoming crispy.

NOTES:

I used peanut oil because of its high smoke point and...well....taste and I will advise you
to do the same or better still use oils like Grapeseed, Canola, Corn, Olive, Sesame seed
or Sunflower.

Also, if you want to make this recipe healthier, you can bake the chicken in the oven at
400 degrees Fahrenheit for about 35-45 minutes or until the internal temperature reaches
165 degrees Fahrenheit. This method will still give you a crispy exterior while reducing the
amount of oil used.

For an extra crispy coating, you can double-dip the chicken by repeating the egg-milk
and flour coating process before frying.

Feel free to adjust the seasoning in the flour mixture to suit your taste preferences.
You can add more spices or herbs to enhance the flavor.

Make sure to monitor the oil temperature throughout the frying process to maintain a consistent
heat. This will help ensure that the chicken cooks evenly and achieves the desired crispiness.


INGREDIENTS:

  • 8 chicken drumsticks
  • 2 cups whole milk
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 teaspoon paprika
  • 1 teaspoon garlic powder
  • 1 teaspoon onion powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon chili powder (optional)
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1/2 teaspoon black pepper
  • Vegetable oil, for frying (such as peanut oil, canola oil, or vegetable oil)

    • Instructions

  • In a large bowl, whisk together the whole milk, eggs, salt, and black pepper. Set aside.
  • In another large bowl, combine the flour, paprika, garlic powder, onion powder, chili powder (if using), salt, and black pepper. Mix well.
  • Dip each chicken drumstick into the flour mixture, coating it well. Then transfer it to the milk mixture, ensuring it's fully coated. Finally, return the drumstick to the flour mixture and coat it thoroughly. For extra crispiness, you can repeat the dipping process.
  • Heat about 2 inches of vegetable oil in a deep fryer or large heavy-bottomed pot to 350°F (175°C).
  • Carefully add the coated chicken drumsticks to the hot oil, being careful not to overcrowd the pot. Fry in batches if necessary.
  • Fry the chicken for about 12-15 minutes, turning occasionally, until the chicken is golden brown and cooked through (internal temperature should reach 165°F or 75°C).
  • Use a slotted spoon or tongs to remove the chicken from the oil and place it on a plate lined with paper towels to drain excess oil.
  • Let the fried chicken rest for a few minutes before serving. Enjoy!

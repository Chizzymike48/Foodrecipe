The Difference In Flaxmeal

I suggest using the golden flax meal instead of the brown variation for your muffins.
Golden flaxseeds have a light, buttery, and slightly sweet taste, while brown flaxseeds have a bolder,
toasted flavor. Using dark brown flax meal will overpower the flavor of your muffins and give them an unusual color.

Ingredients for Making Almond Flour Blueberry
Muffins

Dry ingredients

Almond flour - a nutrient-rich alternative to traditional flour with a nutty flavor and
texture.

Golden Flax meal - binds the ingredients together and gives structure to the muffins.
It also adds a slightly nutty flavor to the muffins.

Monk fruit sweetener - is a low-carb sweetener used as an alternative to sugar.

Salt - enhances the flavor of the muffins.

Baking powder - acts as a leavening agent to make the muffins rise well.


Wet ingredients

Unsweetened plain whole milk yogurt - Opt for unsweetened plain whole milk
yogurt in your recipe.
This ingredient contributes significantly to the moisture and texture of the recipe,
ensuring consistent quality in the final product. Refrain from using
Greek yogurt in this context, as it won't provide sufficient moisture to the mixture
due to its thicker consistency compared to regular yogurt.

Vanilla extract - adds a pleasant vanilla taste.

Eggs - act as a binding agent, add moisture, and aid in leavening.


Add-ins

Fresh or frozen blueberries - provide a burst of flavor and natural sweetness
to your muffins.

How to Make Keto Almond Flour Blueberry
Muffins

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with 12 muffin liners.

Mix the dry ingredients: In a large bowl, combine almond flour, flax meal,
monk fruit sweetener, salt, and baking powder. Mix these dry ingredients well.

Mix the wet ingredients: In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs,
unsweetened yogurt, and vanilla extract.

Combine wet and dry ingredients: Gradually add the wet ingredients to the dry ingredients,
stirring until just combined. Do not overmix.

Fold in the blueberries evenly: Fold in the blueberries, ensuring they are distributed
evenly throughout the batter.

Fill muffin cups: Scoop the batter into the lined muffin cups, filling each about ⅔ of
the way full.

Bake: for 25 to 30 minutes, or until the tops are golden and a toothpick inserted into
the center of a muffin comes out clean.

Cool: Let the muffins cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then remove them and let them
cool completely on a wire rack.


How to Store Keto Almond Flour Blueberry Muffins

To store Keto Almond flour Blueberry Muffins, cool them completely. Then, wrap them
in plastic wrap and place them in an airtight container.

Store them at room temperature for a few days, refrigerate them for up to a week,
or freeze them for up to a few months.

Thaw the muffins when ready to eat.

Ingredients

2 cups almond flour

½ cup golden flax meal

⅔ cup whole milk yogurt unsweetened

½ cup monk fruit sweetener

2 teaspoons vanilla extract

3 large eggs

¼ teaspoon salt

2 teaspoons baking powder

2 teaspoons baking powder

1 cup fresh or frozen blueberries

Instructions

Preheat your oven to 350°F (175°C) and line a muffin tin with 12 muffin
liners.

In a large bowl, combine almond flour, flax meal, monk fruit sweetener,
salt, and baking powder. Mix these dry ingredients well.

In a separate bowl, whisk together the eggs, unsweetened yogurt, and
vanilla extract.

Gradually add the wet ingredients into the dry ingredients, stirring until
just combined. Do not overmix.

Fold in the blueberries, ensuring they are distributed evenly throughout
the batter.

Scoop the batter into the lined muffin cups, filling each about ⅔ of the
way full.

Bake for 20 to 25 minutes, or until the tops are golden and a toothpick
inserted into the center of a muffin comes out clean.

Let the muffins cool in the pan for 5 minutes, then remove them and let
them cool completely on a wire rack.



Notes

Choose blanched almond flour for its finer texture and lighter texture.

Consider combining almond flour and a small amount of coconut flour to
enhance texture and flavor.

Use fresh and frozen blueberries, but frozen blueberries should be coated
with almond flour to prevent moisture.

Choose a keto-friendly sweetener like erythritol, stevia, or monk fruit,
and adjust the amount according to your taste preferences.
